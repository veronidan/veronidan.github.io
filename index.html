Some Older Buildings Are More Energy-efficient than Some Newer Buildings

Older Buildings with Good Efficiency Ratings

The Chrysler Building (built 1930) in New York has an EPA Energy Star rating of 84/100. A score of at least 75 is required for a building to be considered "high efficiency".

Photo by: David Shankbone, modified by: Overand

Newer Buildings with Poor Efficiency Ratings

The Seagram Building (built 1958) in New York has an EPA Energy Star rating of 3/100.


Photo by: Noroton

The Seagram building is worse largely because all of its glass is single-glazed (i.e., one layer thick).
